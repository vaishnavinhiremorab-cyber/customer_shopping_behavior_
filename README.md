# customer_shopping_behavior_
🛍️ Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis
This project uses Python, SQL, and Power BI to analyze 3,900 retail transactions, uncovering key spending trends and customer loyalty segments. It provides actionable insights to help retailers optimize marketing and grow subscription revenue. 

🎯 Project Overview
Welcome! This project explores the shopping habits of 3,900 customers to help a retail business make smarter decisions. By analyzing everything from age groups to shipping preferences, I’ve identified exactly where the revenue is coming from and how to grow it even further. 

🛠️ The Data Stack
I built this project using a powerful "Big Three" workflow: Python for heavy-duty data cleaning, PostgreSQL for deep-dive SQL analysis, and Power BI to turn those numbers into an interactive dashboard. This setup ensures the data is accurate, searchable, and easy for anyone to understand at a glance.

🐍 Data Engineering with Python
First, I used Python to handle the "messy" parts of the data, like filling in 37 missing product ratings using category medians. I also performed Feature Engineering to group customers into age brackets and calculated how often they shop, allowing us to see patterns that weren't obvious in the raw data. 

💾 Business Intelligence via SQL
With SQL, I dug into the business side and found some major wins. For instance, while male customers bring in the most total revenue ($157,890), female shoppers actually spend slightly more on average per trip! I also identified that over 3,100 customers are already "Loyal," meaning the business has a very strong foundation of repeat buyers. 

📊 Visualizing Results in Power BI
The final step was creating a Power BI dashboard that acts as a business "command center." It tracks key metrics like the $59.76 average spend and shows that only 27% of customers currently have a subscription. This reveals a massive opportunity to turn those regular shoppers into committed monthly members. 

🚀 Strategic Growth Recommendations
Based on the data, I recommend targeting "Young Adults" and "Middle-aged" groups with personalized ads, as they are the highest revenue contributors. We should also offer special perks to our loyal non-subscribers to close the "subscription gap" and ensure steady, long-term growth.
